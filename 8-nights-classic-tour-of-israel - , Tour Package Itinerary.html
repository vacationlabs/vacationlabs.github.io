Tour Highlights - 8 Nights Classic Tour Of Israel (Travel )

Share

Print  Share

(Take our Travel Expert's Help)

 
The other side of Israel (unlike its news hour persona) is fascinating to boot. It is rightly envisioned as a bridge between Africa, Asia and Europe. A pint sized country which packs quite a punch! On this trip you would experience a bubbling melting pot - of region, religion and natures beauty. Let the pages of history leap out and thrill you! 
 
From the dizzying and historic heights of Massada to the strategically located Golan Heights, from the Sea of Galillee to the Dead Sea, from the ultra orthodox neighbourhood of Jerusalem to the glitzy, vibrant nightlife of Tel-Aviv, this trip is a perfectly knit tapestry of tradition and authenticity of this land.Introduction to Israel was never this deep and throbbing. We have even included night stays at a Kibbutz an experience of an alternate lifestyle indigenous to 
Israel! 
 
*All our scheduled trips are fixed date departures where you arrive in this mystic land on a Tuesday. For the concerned departure dates browse below in the Fixed Departure Dates section.

Itinerary details for "8 Nights Classic Tour Of Israel tour package"
 

Day 1: Tuesday Jerusalem

Arrive at the Ben Gurion Airport and say Shalom to our local representative who will escort you and help you to transfer to your hotel. Jerusalem will give a tug at your spiritual core, irrespective of any religion you practice. This Holy City plays host to thousands of year of history, tradition and three Abrahamic faiths: Judaism, Islam and Christianity. Spend the night in Jerusalem.
 
Day 2: Wednesday - Jerusalem: Old City
 
Start your day with a magnificent panoramic city-spread view from Mount of Olives, on top of the Austrian Hospice. Then drive to the Old City to visit the tomb of King David and the room of Last Supper on Mt. Zion.  Steeped in sight, sound, smell and other sensory overload, Old City is the heart of Jerusalem. Let the 16th century Ottoman walls make you gasp with wonder as you enter the walled city and walk through the newly restored Jewish quarter.  Next visit the Roman Cardo, the Herodian quarter and continue your walk to the Western Wall.  After tucking in your prayer (if so you wish) in the cracks of the Wailing Wall, walk along the Via Dolorosa to the church of the Holy Sepulchre. Feel the magic of this ancient church as you wander within it. 
Pay a visit to Palestine as you set out to witness the birthplace of Jesus next in Bethlehem* on the West Bank. See Rachel's Tomb and proceed to visit the Church of Nativity and Manger's Square. After an overwhelming spiritual excursion spend the night in Jerusalem.
 
Day 3: Thursday - Jerusalem: New City
 
Today you set out to discover the triumphant rise of a modern metropolis right from the ashes of the Holocaust. Drive to the Israel Museum to visit the Shrine of the Book where the Dead Sea Scrolls are housed. Also check out the Second Temple period miniature model of Jerusalem, built on a 50:1 scale incorporating even minute detailing. Later proceed for a tour at the Knesset, Israel's parliament. Pay your obeisance at the Mount Hertzel cemetery, a burial ground of many eminent dignitaries and powerful politicians of Israel. It also honours Israels martyrs and victims of terrorism. A short walk down from their will take you to Yad Vashem, the Holocaust History Museum. To learn about the unique lifestyle of the resident Jews of Jerusalem, visit Mea Shearim, the Hasidic Orthodox Jewish neighborhood. Their ways might be conservative (to us) but their hearts are very warm. In the evening enjoy a Middle Eastern dinner followed by the spectacular "sound & light" show* at the Tower of David museum (weather conditions permitting).
Break for the night in Jerusalem.
 
Day 4: Friday Qumran / Massada / Dead Sea
 
Today enjoy a drive through the Judean Desert to the Dead Sea, the lowest point on earth which is almost 1300 feet (400 meters) below sea level. Make a brief stop at Qumran to visit the site where the Dead Sea Scrolls were found. Drive along the shores of the Dead Sea to Massada. Ascend by cable car as a stunning desert view unfolds around you. Take a tour of the ancient fortress where the Zealots made their last stand against the Romans before committing mass suicide in 73 A.D. Descend by cable car. Drive down to the Dead Sea to luxuriate in  worlds largest spa. Slather yourself with the mineral rich mud and experience a float in the Dead Sea* that many believe has its therapeutic effects (*weather permitting). Youll now  understand why German and Austrian health insurance plans actually pay to have their citizens with skin diseases fly to the Dead Sea for treatment!
 After a soak and a float, get back on the road to reach Tel Aviv and spend your night there.
 
 Day 5: Saturday Tel Aviv
 
Observe Shabbat during the day and witness a change in pace of life around you. After sunset, take the pulsating city of Tel Aviv by a storm and explore it on your own. From pub hopping to lounging at a glamorous night club Tel Aviv has plenty to offer. Spend the day at leisure. Retire for the night in Tel Aviv.
 
 
Day 6: Sunday - Caesarea / Megiddo / Druze Villages
 
Take to the road for a drive along the coastal plain to Caesarea, capital of Judea under the Romans. See the excavations of the Crusader city, Roman port, aqueduct and the amphitheater that has been restored to its former glory as a concert venue. Proceed to Megiddo, identified as the site of Armageddon; visit the archaeological excavations including the well preserved water supply system. Continue to Daliat El Carmel, a Druze village and enjoy a late lunch at a local restaurant.  Dont forget to try lafa made on a taboon! Get northward bound to explore the coastal range of Mount Carmel and the port city of Haifa. The city of Haifa exists from the base of the biblical Mount Carmel all the way to the top.  Stop for a panoramic view of the Haifa Bay and the Golden domed Bahai shrine (UNESCO World Heritage site) and its breathtaking Gardens.
Tonight, enjoy the pleasures of a communal settlement in a Kibbutz guesthouse and get introduced to a unique philosophy of harmonious living.
 
Day 7: Monday - Safed / Golan Heights / Winery
 
Start for Safed today to get soaked in the mystic aura of this tiny town which is one of Judaisms four holy sites and the center of Kabbalah. Match your rhythms with its winding cobblestone alleys and visit the ancient synagogues and artists colony.  From the tenets of religion, skip next to the politically prime location of Golan. It shares its border with three of Israels neighbours Jordan, Syria and Lebanon. Visit Golan Heights to see the former Syrian bunkers, Kuneitra and the new Israeli town of Katzrin. Later stop at the Golan Winery for a tour and taste of delicious Israeli wine. Enjoy the unfolding striking scenery and pastoral atmosphere around the winery. Later stop at Kessem Hagolan for a multimedia presentation of the area and its history. Retire for night at the Kibbutz guesthouse. 
 
Day 8: Tuesday - Sea of Galilee / Nazareth
 
As you approach Nazareth to begin your daylong tryst with Biblical miracles, you will observe how mosques and churches have started replacing synagogues. Visit the Church of the Annunciation standing over the cave which tradition holds to be the home of the Virgin Mary. Travel to Tiberias and visit Capernaum to see the ruins of the synagogue and octagonal Church of St. Peter. Visit the Mount of Beatitudes where Jesus delivered the Sermon on the Mount.  Cross the Jordan River to enter the Beit Shean valley. Visit the ancient synagogue of Beit Alpha to see the 1200 years old mosaic floor with depiction of the zodiac signs.
Break for the night at Tel Aviv.
 
Day 9: Wednesday - Departure
 
From Tel Aviv/ Ben Gurion Airport
After breakfast you will be transferred to Ben Gurion Airport for departure. Bid adieu to this land which forever will stand for a beautiful collage that of cultures, religions, ethnicities and history.
